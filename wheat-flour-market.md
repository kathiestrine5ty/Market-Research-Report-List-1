<p><ul><li>Self Raising Flour</li><li>Bread Flour</li><li>Biscuit Flour</li><li>Cake Flour</li><li>Whole Meal Flour</li><li>Resultant Flour</li><li>Semolina</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The wheat flour market encompasses various types tailored for specific baking needs. Self-raising flour contains baking powder for quick leavening, ideal for cakes and pancakes. Bread flour has higher protein content for chewy loaves, while biscuit flour is finely milled for tender biscuits. Cake flour is low in protein for soft, airy cakes. Whole meal flour includes the entire grain for nutritional richness, whereas resultant flour combines different flours for versatile use. Semolina, coarsely milled wheat, is used in pasta and certain baked goods.</p></p>
