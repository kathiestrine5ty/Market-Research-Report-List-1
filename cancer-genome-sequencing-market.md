<p><strong>The Cancer Genome Sequencing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Second Generation</li><li>Third Generation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cancer genome sequencing market is primarily categorized into second-generation and third-generation sequencing technologies. Second-generation sequencing, known as next-generation sequencing (NGS), enables high-throughput sequencing at reduced costs, focusing on short-read sequences, suitable for various cancer research applications. Third-generation sequencing, on the other hand, offers long-read capabilities that enhance the resolution of complex genomic regions, enabling improved structural variation detection and more comprehensive insights into cancer genomes. Both technologies are vital for advancing personalized medicine and targeted therapies in oncology.</p></p>
