<p><p>The bimetallic bandsaw blade market encompasses two primary types: high-speed steel (HSS) band saw blades and carbide-tipped band saw blades. HSS blades are known for their durability and flexibility, making them ideal for cutting various materials, particularly softer metals. In contrast, carbide-tipped blades feature tips made of carbide, offering superior hardness and wear resistance, making them suitable for cutting harder materials such as stainless steel and high-strength alloys. Each type serves distinct applications based on material properties and cutting requirements.</p></p>
