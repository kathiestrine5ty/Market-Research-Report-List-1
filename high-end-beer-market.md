<p><p>The high-end beer market consists of three main types: ale, lager, and hybrid beers. Ale beers, known for their robust flavors and fruity aromas, are brewed with top-fermenting yeast at warmer temperatures. Lager beers, in contrast, use bottom-fermenting yeast at cooler temperatures, resulting in a crisp, clean taste. Hybrid beers blend characteristics of both ales and lagers, showcasing unique flavors and fermentation techniques. Together, these types cater to diverse consumer preferences, driving innovation and premium offerings in the craft beer segment.</p></p>
