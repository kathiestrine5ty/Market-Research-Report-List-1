<p><p>The medical publishing market consists of two primary types: Open Access and Subscription-based models. The Open Access model allows unrestricted access to research articles, enabling broader dissemination and collaboration among researchers and the public, often funded through article processing charges. In contrast, the Subscription-based model requires institutions or individuals to pay for access to journals, limiting readership but providing publishers with consistent revenue streams. Both models significantly shape how medical research is shared and accessed within the scientific community.</p></p>
