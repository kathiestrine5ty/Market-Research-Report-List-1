<p><p>The renewable fiber market includes various types such as regenerated cellulose fiber, regenerated protein fiber, and polynosic fiber. Regenerated cellulose fiber, derived from natural sources like wood pulp, is known for its biodegradability and moisture-wicking properties. Regenerated protein fiber, made from renewable protein sources, offers unique elasticity and softness. Polynosic fiber, a variant of regenerated cellulose, combines the benefits of modal and Tencel, providing enhanced luster and breathability. Together, these fibers promote sustainable textile alternatives.</p></p>
