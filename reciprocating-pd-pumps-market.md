<p><p>Reciprocating positive displacement (PD) pumps are categorized into three main types: plunger pumps, piston pumps, and diaphragm pumps. Plunger pumps utilize a cylindrical plunger to create pressure, making them suitable for high-pressure applications. Piston pumps operate similarly but use pistons for fluid movement, offering versatility across various fluids and pressures. Diaphragm pumps employ a flexible diaphragm to create a seal and move fluid, ideal for handling corrosive substances and providing safer operation. Each type serves distinct applications based on operational needs.</p></p>
