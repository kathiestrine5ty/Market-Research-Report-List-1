<p><p>Ferrite bonded magnets are divided into two primary market types: injected magnets and pressed magnets. Injected magnets are produced by combining ferrite powder with a thermoplastic binder, allowing for complex shapes and higher precision in smaller sizes, making them suitable for applications requiring intricate designs. Pressed magnets, on the other hand, are formed by compressing ferrite powder in a mold, ensuring durability and stability, often used in larger applications where strength is paramount. Each type serves unique application demands in various industries.</p></p>
