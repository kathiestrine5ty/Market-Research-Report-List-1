<p><p>The rechargeable battery market comprises several key types. Lithium-ion (LiOn) batteries are favored for their high energy density and lightweight nature, commonly used in smartphones and electric vehicles. Nickel-Metal Hydride (NiMH) batteries offer good capacity and are often found in hybrid vehicles and consumer electronics. Nickel-Cadmium (NiCad) batteries, known for their durability and resistance to extreme temperatures, have declined in popularity due to environmental concerns. Other rechargeable batteries include technologies like lithium polymer and lead-acid, catering to various applications.</p></p>
