<p><ul><li>Spark-Ignition Engine</li><li>Compression-Ignition Engine</li><li>Gas-Turbine Engine</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The ammonia-fuelled engine market encompasses three main types: spark-ignition engines, compression-ignition engines, and gas-turbine engines. Spark-ignition engines use a spark to ignite a fuel-air mixture, offering efficient combustion for lighter loads. Compression-ignition engines, or diesel engines, rely on high pressures to ignite ammonia, suitable for heavy-duty applications. Gas-turbine engines utilize ammonia in a high-temperature, high-pressure environment to produce thrust, commonly employed in power generation and aviation. Each type presents unique advantages and applications in transitioning to cleaner energy.</p></p>
