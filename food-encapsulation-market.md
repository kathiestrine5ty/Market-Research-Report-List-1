<p><p>The food encapsulation market features three main types: microencapsulation, nanoencapsulation, and hybrid encapsulation. Microencapsulation involves enclosing food ingredients within microparticles to enhance stability and control release, while nanoencapsulation uses nanotechnology to create smaller, more effective delivery systems, improving bioavailability and flavor retention. Hybrid encapsulation combines both micro- and nano-encapsulation techniques, leveraging the advantages of each for improved functionality and efficiency in food products. Together, these methods enhance flavor, nutrition, and shelf life in the food industry.</p></p>
