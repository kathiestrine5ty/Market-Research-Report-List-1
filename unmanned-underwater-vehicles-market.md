<p><p>Unmanned Underwater Vehicles (UUVs) are primarily categorized into Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs) and Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs). AUVs are designed for autonomous missions, equipped with advanced sensors and GPS, suitable for tasks like environmental monitoring and data collection. In contrast, ROVs are remotely controlled, allowing operators to conduct real-time observations and interventions in deep-sea environments, often used in oil and gas exploration or underwater inspection. Both types play crucial roles in marine research, industry, and defense applications.</p></p>
