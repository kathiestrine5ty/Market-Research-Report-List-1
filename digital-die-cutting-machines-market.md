<p><p>Digital die cutting machines are categorized into software-based and cartridge-based models. Software-based die cut machines utilize design software to create custom patterns and shapes, allowing for versatility and ease of use in various projects. In contrast, cartridge-based die cut machines rely on pre-made cartridges that contain specific designs, making them simpler but less flexible. Both types cater to different user needs, with software-based machines appealing to those seeking creativity and customization, while cartridge-based models suit users preferring straightforward operations.</p></p>
