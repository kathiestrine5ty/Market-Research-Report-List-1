<p><p>The spiral implant market is primarily categorized into two types: conical and cylindrical implants. Conical implants feature a tapered design that enhances stability and allows for easier placement in varying bone densities. They are often preferred for their superior retention and adaptability to the surrounding anatomy. In contrast, cylindrical implants have a uniform diameter, which provides a consistent surface area for osseointegration. They are widely used for their straightforward insertion and predictable outcomes, appealing to a range of clinical applications.</p></p>
