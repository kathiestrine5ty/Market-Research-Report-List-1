<p><p>The edible animal fat market is categorized into three primary types: liquid, solid, and semi-solid. Liquid animal fats, such as lard and rendered beef fat, are commonly used in cooking and food processing due to their high smoke points. Solid fats, like suet, are typically used in baking and can impart rich flavors. Semi-solid fats, such as shortening, offer versatility for both frying and baking, providing a balance between stability and ease of use in various culinary applications.</p></p>
