<p><p>The Extruded Polylactic Acid (PLA) Fiber Market encompasses three main types based on raw material sources: tapioca, sugarcane, and cornstarch. Tapioca-made PLA fibers leverage starch from cassava roots, offering a biodegradable and eco-friendly option. Sugarcane-based PLA utilizes fermentable sugars derived from cane, promoting sustainability. Cornstarch-made PLA fibers are derived from corn kernels, widely available and cost-effective. Each type contributes to the growing demand for sustainable materials in textiles, packaging, and various applications, reflecting the shift towards environmentally friendly products.</p></p>
