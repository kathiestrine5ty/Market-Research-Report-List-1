<p><p>Indwelling Pleural Catheters (IPCs) are medical devices used for the management of pleural effusions, specifically designed to facilitate fluid drainage from the pleural cavity. These catheters are increasingly used in patients with chronic effusions, providing a more prolonged solution compared to traditional thoracostomy tubes. The rising prevalence of conditions such as heart failure, cancer, and pneumonia has spurred demand for IPCs, which can enhance patients' quality of life by reducing hospital stays and the need for frequent procedures.</p><p>The Indwelling Pleural Catheters Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.40% during the forecast period, driven by advancements in catheter technology and growing awareness of IPC benefits. The market is also seeing trends like increased adoption of minimally invasive procedures, a shift towards outpatient settings, and the development of innovative catheter designs that enhance usability and safety. Additionally, the aging population and improvements in healthcare infrastructure contribute to market expansion, while ongoing research and clinical trials continue to validate the efficacy and safety of IPCs, further driving their adoption in clinical practice.
