<p><p>The Medical Elastomers market is divided into two main types: Organic and Inorganic Medical Elastomers. Organic Medical Elastomers are primarily composed of synthetic polymers such as silicone and polyurethane, offering excellent flexibility, biocompatibility, and resistance to chemicals, making them ideal for applications like medical devices and implants. In contrast, Inorganic Medical Elastomers, often based on materials like rubber or thermoplastic elastomers, provide added durability and thermal stability, suited for products requiring robust performance under extreme conditions. Both types play crucial roles in advancing healthcare solutions.</p></p>
