<p><p>Gunfire locators for defense utilize various technologies to detect and identify the source of gunfire. Acoustic systems use microphones to capture sound waves, triangulating the location based on the time difference of arrivals. They are effective in detecting multiple gunshots over a broad area. Optical systems, on the other hand, rely on visual sensors to identify the flash or trajectory of the projectile. They provide precise targeting information and are particularly useful in low-visibility environments. Both play crucial roles in enhancing situational awareness and response.</p></p>
