<p><p>Metal detectors for the frozen food market typically fall into three main types: Pulse Induction, Very Low Frequency, and Beat-Frequency Oscillation. Pulse Induction is effective for detecting non-ferrous metals and operates by sending short pulses of current. Very Low Frequency detectors use low-frequency signals, allowing for better sensitivity to various metals while minimizing interference. Beat-Frequency Oscillation combines two frequencies to improve detection accuracy, making it suitable for identifying contaminants in frozen food products. Each type caters to specific detection needs in the industry.</p></p>
